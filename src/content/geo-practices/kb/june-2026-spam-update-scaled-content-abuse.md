## Summary
Google's June 24-26 2026 Spam Update extended SpamBrain to AI Overviews and AI Mode — tactics designed to manipulate AI answers are now treated as spam, equivalent to paid links. "Scaled content abuse" triggers on sites producing large volumes of content solely to influence AI answers without adding genuine value.

## Details
The update's "scaled content abuse" policy targets sites producing large volumes of content solely to influence AI answers without providing genuine value. This affects both traditional search and AI Overviews/AI Mode citations. The key risk: GEO tactics that manufacture content for AI citation without real utility — e.g., thousands of FAQ pages targeting every possible AI query — now carry spam penalty risk. The update signals Google's intent to prevent AI search manipulation the same way it prevents traditional search spam. Quality, uniqueness, and genuine value remain the only safe scaling strategies.
