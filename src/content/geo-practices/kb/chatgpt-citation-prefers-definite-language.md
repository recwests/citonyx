## Summary
Growth Memo (February 2026) identified ChatGPT's citation preferences: content with definite language (not hedging), question-form headings, high entity density, balanced facts with opinions, and simple writing. Early-discovery content with 5-7 statistics gets 20% higher citation likelihood (AirOps, April 2026).

## Details
ChatGPT's citation patterns differ from Google's ranking criteria. It favors: (1) definite, authoritative claims over hedged or balanced language, (2) question-form H2s that match prompt structures, (3) content rich in named entities (brands, people, places, concepts), (4) a mix of factual data with editorial opinion, and (5) simple, readable prose over complex academic writing. The AirOps finding that 5-7 statistics per section boosts citation likelihood by 20% reinforces the data-rich content strategy. For the Citonyx testbed: structure content with definite claims, question headings, high entity density, and embedded statistics.
