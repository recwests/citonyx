## Summary
Ahrefs found that only 37.9% of pages cited in Google AI Overviews also rank in the top 10 organic results as of March 2026, down from 76% in August 2025. Being #1 in Google no longer guarantees AI Overview citation. AI citations have decoupled from traditional ranking as an independent optimization target.

## Details
The 38 percentage point collapse in 7 months represents the fastest decoupling of traditional and AI search metrics ever documented. The mechanism: AI Overviews use query fan-out (8-16 sub-queries per query), naturally diversifying sources beyond what traditional ranking serves. DerivateX study (100 software-buying queries, 1,259 citations, June-July 2026) confirmed 35% source overlap — YouTube cited in 51/100 AIO queries but ranked in only 7, while Reddit ranked in 82 queries but cited in only 43. 63.4% of AIO citations came from third-party listicles.

Ahrefs updated analysis (863K keywords, 4M AIO URLs, July 2026): only 38% of AI Overview citations come from top-10 organic results. Pages ranking 11-100 account for 31.2%; beyond rank 100 another 31%. WhatsMyGeoScore July 2026 analysis of 8,500 AI Overviews across Google, ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Claude confirms the divergence: informational queries 38-47% from top-10, commercial 45-54%, transactional 52-61%, local 29-41%. Perplexity shows the weakest correlation (31% from top 10). Google's May 2026 Core Update explicitly prioritized 'content structure and extractability signals' for AIO inclusion — correlating with a 34% increase in citations from positions 11-50.

Superlines research (May 2026): only ~17% average overlap between AI-cited sources and organic top-10 across platforms. Only 11% of domains shared between Google Search and ChatGPT for the same query. Average AI Overview now cites 4.1 unique domains, down from 6.2 in early 2025 — fewer slots, higher competition. The strategic implication: citation share and organic rank are distinct KPIs with only 0.34 correlation. Content optimized solely for Google ranking is increasingly invisible to AI systems. Brands with strong organic rankings but poor answer-first content quality lose AI visibility.
