## Summary
AirOps analyzed 16,851 ChatGPT search queries to measure the position-to-citation rate curve. The top retrieval result was cited approximately 58% of the time, while position 10 dropped to ~14%. Narrow, focused pages answering one specific question consistently and significantly outperformed broad "ultimate guides" by a wide margin.

## Details
The 58%→14% decay curve shows ChatGPT's citation mechanism heavily front-loads sources, similar to its general LLM citation pattern (44% front-loaded in earlier studies). Pages structured as single-answer deep dives with entity names, comparison structures, and extractable statements outperform comprehensive guides. Recommended page structure: definition → short answer → comparison table → evidence → detailed explanation → FAQ. Pages that directly answer one precise question with data tables and numbered comparisons see highest citation rates. This creates a strategic tension with traditional SEO, which often rewards breadth over depth.
