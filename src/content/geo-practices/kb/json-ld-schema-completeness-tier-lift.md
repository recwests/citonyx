## Summary
Toronto SEO 2026 study: pages with full JSON-LD schema coverage (Article+FAQPage+Person, or equivalent richness) are cited at 2-3x the rate of schema-thin equivalents, controlling for ranking position and content depth. Perplexity shows largest schema lift; Gemini shows smallest. FAQPage delivers the highest single-type lift.
## Details
Schema types ranked by citation lift: FAQPage (highest single-type), Article+Person combined (compounds author E-E-A-T), HowTo (on procedural content), Product+AggregateRating (ecommerce). BreadcrumbList and standalone Review show low direct lift. Schema lift varies by engine: Perplexity highest sensitivity, Google AI Overviews high, ChatGPT moderate-high, Gemini moderate, Copilot low. Critical finding: schema citation lift is not "set and forget" — pages with drifted/invalid schema showed citation rate decay even when content quality held steady. Recommend quarterly schema validation. This complements Growth Marshal's finding that attribute-rich schema (fully populated) outperforms generic, and also aligns with Ahrefs' finding that generic schema alone provides no lift.
