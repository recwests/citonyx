## Summary
Freshness signal sharply intensified in 2026. GitHub study (May 2026, 153K citations, 5K queries, 6 platforms) found median cited content age dropped from 2.2 years to 298 days. Cross-platform study of 2.4M AI responses found 73% of cited pages were updated within the previous 6 months. Ahrefs 17M URL study confirms AI engines cite content 25.7% fresher than Google organic top-10. Erlin data across 500+ brands: content under 3 months averages 48% AI coverage. The staleness penalty runs at ~1.8% coverage lost per month of inactivity.

## Details
Ahrefs 17M citation study by platform: ChatGPT direct citations avg 2.6y, Google AI Overviews 3.9y — a 15-month gap. Perplexity avg 3.2y, Gemini 3.1y, Copilot 2.9y. Zyppy meta-analysis (54 studies) confirms the "25.7% fresher" claim is reliable across studies. Content Intelligence Lab (April 2026) cross-platform study of 2.4M AI responses found 73% of cited pages were updated within 6 months, and 65% of AI bot traffic targets content updated within the last year. Superlines reported 28% citation lift on recently updated pages. Erlin data quantifies the penalty: content under 3 months = 48% coverage, 3-6 months = 39%, 6-12 months = 31%, 12-24 months = 23%, over 24 months = 18%. Brands updating monthly see ~23% higher coverage. Recommended cadence: cornerstone content refresh every 60 days with updated data and clear Last Updated timestamps. Perplexity weights freshness most heavily. Adding a single new data point with a clear timestamp is enough to maintain freshness signals. Article schema with both publishedDate and modifiedDate signals recency — both engines weight heavily. However, the widely repeated "3.2x more citations within 30 days" stat does not replicate — it appears to be a single-vendor finding. Freshness helps but is not the dominant factor; brand mentions and entity authority still rank higher.
