## Summary
Discovered Labs regression analysis found prompt-content alignment (how directly content answers the likely user query) is the strongest single predictor of AI citation (β=+0.37) — more impactful than domain authority. Content structured around direct answers gets cited most.

## Details
This shifts content strategy from keyword-optimized to prompt-optimized. Content should be structured around direct questions and answers, use clear H2s with question phrasing, and front-load key information. The finding aligns with Magna AI's 1.8x citation lift from question-form H2 headings. Practical implementation: research the actual prompts users enter in AI tools (not just Google keywords), structure content as direct answers to those prompts, use definition-first openings, and support claims with structured data. Prompt alignment rewards concision and specificity over comprehensive but unfocused content.
