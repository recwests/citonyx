## Summary
Trakkr's 5 proprietary studies (2026): GPTBot averages 60.5 pages per crawl session, 29% more active on weekends. Only 0.17% of prompts reach ChatGPT's search as typed — the model aggressively rewrites queries before searching. ChatGPT agrees with other AI models on #1 recommendation only 43.9% of the time. Citation frequency follows a power law — a small number of sources capture most citations.

## Details
Practical implications: (1) Publish fresh content on Thursday/Friday for weekend crawls (higher GPTBot activity Saturday/Sunday). (2) Optimize for rewritten query intent, not exact-match keywords — the model transforms queries before retrieval. (3) Since ChatGPT diverges from other AI models >56% of the time on top recommendations, an engine-specific optimization strategy is required. Content that ranks #1 in Google has only ~44% chance of being ChatGPT's top recommendation. Over 100M weekly ChatGPT users ask product and brand questions.
