## Summary
Burson and Profound's June 2026 study of 85 companies (55,000+ believability forecasts) identified the 'credibility paradox': being cited in AI answers does not guarantee being believed. Visibility and credibility are separate scoreboards requiring distinct measurement.

## Details
A brand can appear in AI answers everywhere yet still lose if the model frames it in a way that does not earn trust. Sentiment and factual accuracy belong alongside reach in any serious GEO measurement setup. This reframes GEO from a pure visibility play to a reputation strategy — brands must monitor not just whether they are cited, but how they are characterized in AI outputs and whether the characterization builds or erodes trust.
