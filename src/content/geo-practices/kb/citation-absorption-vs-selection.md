## Summary
Two-layer AI citation market: "citation selection" (page named as source) vs "citation absorption" (facts/wording used in answer). A cited page with weak absorption has low commercial value. Dense formatting, comparison tables, pricing matrices, and numbered workflows increase absorption and are harder for AI to paraphrase away.

## Details
This distinction is critical for B2B brands: a pricing matrix with documented limits is more absorbable than "contact sales for details." AI systems prefer passages extractable without interpretation — tables, numbered workflows, definitions. The best GEO pages look less like blog posts and more like machine-readable research files written for human executives. Measuring both selection and absorption requires sampling actual AI answers, not just citation logs. Absorption rate may be a better KPI than raw citation count for commercial pages.
