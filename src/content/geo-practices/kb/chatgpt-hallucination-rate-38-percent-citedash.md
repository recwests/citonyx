## Summary
CiteDash's 2026 benchmark of ~3,400 academic citations found ChatGPT hallucinates ~38% of references — inventing plausible DOIs and real authors on fabricated papers. Claude hallucinates ~24%, Gemini ~19%, and Perplexity only ~8%, making Perplexity the most citation-accurate engine.

## Details
The hallucination problem is particularly severe for brands cited in academic or research-heavy contexts. ChatGPT tends to generate convincing-sounding but entirely fabricated citations — including real author names attached to invented papers and DOIs that resolve to different content. Perplexity's low 8% rate reflects its RAG pipeline that retrieves and cites retrievable web pages. For brands wanting accurate citations, structuring content for Perplexity's retrieval pipeline provides the most reliable citation path.
