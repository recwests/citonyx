## Summary
BrightEdge analyzed 50K AI Overview citations: 71% come from pages ranking organic positions 1-12. But 29% come from outside top 12 through content differentiation — original research (34% of outliers), verifiable expert authorship (28%), unique case studies (19%), structured data differentiation (12%), and unique comparison/aggregation (7%).
## Details
AI Overviews seek source diversity — if only your page has a specific statistic, it gets cited regardless of position. BrightEdge also found 96.8% of cited domains saw zero change week-over-week — citations consolidate, not redistribute. Domain overlap across engines varies widely: institutional sources range 10-26%; UGC ranges 0.2-18%. Gemini: 26% institutional, 0.2% UGC. The 29% outlier window is the primary entry point for brands without established organic rankings. Strategy implication: invest in unique data creation (surveys, benchmarks, original frameworks) that no other source has; this bypasses the ranking prerequisite.
