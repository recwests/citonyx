## Summary
Cloudflare documented that AI crawlers consume content at 38,000x the rate they send referral traffic back. Conductor benchmarks confirm AI referral traffic is just 1.08% of all website traffic, with ChatGPT driving 87.4% of that. Brands must balance crawl accessibility with traffic ROI expectations.

## Details
This massive asymmetry explains why many publishers block training crawlers (GPTBot, CCBot) while allowing search crawlers (ChatGPT-User, PerplexityBot). Bytespider (ByteDance) alone accounts for ~90% of AI crawler traffic on some networks and is known to ignore robots.txt. The divide between "training bots" (consume content, send zero traffic) and "search bots" (provide citations + potential traffic) is the key policy decision for robots.txt configuration in 2026.
