## Summary
The Mind Reader method, published at ACL 2026, introduces decomposition-recombination query augmentation enriching queries with latent semantic information. Decomposes into diverse perspectives and recombines into variants. Reasoning coverage content optimization module aligns content with underlying user search demands. Results: up to 2.44x objective metrics and 1.23x subjective metrics on average.

## Details
Previous GEO methods rely on empirical strategies or query-dependent preferences but overlook latent user search demands. Mind Reader addresses this through two modules: (1) decomposition-recombination query augmentation extracting underlying semantic information, (2) reasoning coverage content optimization covering both query-specific and cross-query shared reasoning information. Experiments on GEO-Bench and new PC-GEO benchmark. Future work plans to extend to cross-modal generative search scenarios.
