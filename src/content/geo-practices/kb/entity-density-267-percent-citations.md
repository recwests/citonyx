## Summary
A 2026 analysis of 21,143 citations finds entity density (clear entity chains, Wikidata/schema links) boosts citation rates by 267% — more than structure or freshness alone. These are engineering properties, not editorial qualities.

## Details
Machine Relations Index research concludes a citation is awarded when a source is (1) accessible, (2) structurally parseable, (3) semantically aligned, (4) factually corroborable, and (5) fresh. Investment hierarchy: crawler accessibility first (binary gate), then structure (2.5× table lift, 28–40% FAQ lift), then entity density (+267%), then freshness (30-day cycle), then cross-source corroboration. Entity chains, structured data, and cross-domain authority predict citation outcomes more reliably than reputation or prose quality.
