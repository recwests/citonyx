## Summary
Ahrefs found that only 37.9% of pages cited in Google AI Overviews also rank in the top 10 organic results as of March 2026, down from 76% in August 2025. Being #1 in Google no longer guarantees AI Overview citation. AI citations have decoupled from traditional ranking as an independent optimization target.

## Details
The 38 percentage point collapse in 7 months represents the fastest decoupling of traditional and AI search metrics ever documented. The mechanism: AI Overviews use query fan-out (8-16 sub-queries per query), naturally diversifying sources beyond what traditional ranking serves. DerivateX study (100 software-buying queries, 1,259 citations, June-July 2026) confirmed 35% source overlap — YouTube cited in 51/100 AIO queries but ranked in only 7, while Reddit ranked in 82 queries but cited in only 43. 63.4% of AIO citations came from third-party listicles. This means content optimized solely for Google ranking is increasingly invisible to AI systems — and vice versa. Brands with strong organic rankings but poor answer-first content quality lose AI visibility. Publishers tracking only Google Search Console see a shrinking portion of their total search footprint. The overlap continues declining.
