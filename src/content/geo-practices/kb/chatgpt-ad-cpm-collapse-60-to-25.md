## Summary
OpenAI launched ChatGPT advertisements at $60 CPM in February 2026, reaching $100M annualized revenue within six weeks. By April 2026, the platform shifted to cost-per-click bidding. CPMs collapsed from $60 to $25 in ten weeks as supply expanded.

## Details
The rapid CPM compression signals a premium land-grab evolving into a performance channel faster than typical ad platform maturation. For GEO practitioners: ChatGPT's ad product creates a parallel visibility path — paid placement inside AI responses may complement organic citation strategies. However, the accelerating CPM decline mirrors traditional ad platform patterns where early premium pricing normalizes downward. Perplexity took the opposite approach, abandoning ads entirely for a subscription-only model (2026). Anthropic's Claude maintains a hard line: no advertising, no sponsored content, no product placements. Platform monetization strategies are diverging, affecting long-term visibility economics.
