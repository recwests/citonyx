## Summary
Deepak Gupta's GEO Measurement Study (Feb-May 2026): 240-page corpus across 4 sites, 200 prompts, 6 engines, biweekly pulls for 13 weeks. Product-style pages (vendor profiles, comparison tables, methodology references) took 38,200 citations (76%) while blog posts took 12,231 (24%), even though blogs made up ~40% of the corpus. Pages with clean H1-H2-H3 hierarchy were 2.8x more likely to earn a citation.

## Details
AI engines reward pages that answer a query inside one self-contained, structured block. Product pages deliver this by default (comparison table, spec list, pricing grid, methodology section). Blog posts bury answers under narrative intro. By query type: comparison and buyer-intent prompts pulled product pages. Implementation prompts pulled how-to and methodology pages. Definitional prompts pulled reference-style pages. Pages built to answer a specific question in a structured block beat pages built to tell a story.
