## Summary
Google's LLM patent suggests a fundamental reframing: the goal is teaching AI who you are, not just ranking pages. AI Overviews cite self-serving listicles but recommend competitors 69% of the time — citation ≠ recommendation. Entity clarity determines whether citation leads to recommendation.

## Details
The patent indicates Google's LLMs assess entity understanding — does the system know who you are, what you do, and whether to trust you — as a signal when selecting sources. The gap between citation and recommendation is now measurable. To close it: invest in entity clarity (Wikidata QID, sameAs schema, consistent NAP), E-E-A-T signals (author credentials, editorial transparency), and genuine differentiation the LLM can articulate. Citation without recommendation is a vanity metric. Knowledge Graph contains 500B+ facts about 5B+ entities. Gemini AI is trained on the Knowledge Graph.
