## Summary
OtterlyAI URL Citation Study (May 2026, 1M+ URLs): URLs with '/guide/' in path average 42% more citations. Analysis of 30M citations (Wellows) shows comparative listicles lead with 32.5% of AI citations across platforms. Content with tables cited 2.5x more. Long-form exceeding 2,000 words earns 3x more citations than short posts. Five formats yield highest citation ROI: structured guides, original research, comparisons, FAQ-dense explainers, entity-rich definitions.

## Details
The five highest-citation-yield formats: (1) structured guides — comprehensive, step-by-step, entity-rich, each section a standalone citation target; (2) original research with proprietary data — achieves primary source status that compounds over time; (3) comparison/alternative pages — best X for Y and Tool A vs Tool B are among highest-volume decision queries; (4) FAQ-dense explainers — each Q&A pair is a standalone citation trigger; (5) entity-rich definitions and glossaries — cited for factual grounding. Thin promotional copy almost never cited. Articles with 15+ verifiable data points receive substantially more AI citations regardless of format.
