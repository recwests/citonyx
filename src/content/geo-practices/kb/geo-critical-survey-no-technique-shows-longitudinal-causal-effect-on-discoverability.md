## Summary
A critical survey of 45 GEO studies (2023–2026) argues GEO is not a single ranking task but a stochastic pipeline. Foundational gains are valid experimentally but conditional on already-retrieved content — no reviewed technique shows stable longitudinal cross-platform causal effect on organic discoverability.

## Details
The survey introduces a multistage formal model separating discoverability, citation, absorption, and economic outcomes. Topical relevance and context position are the most reproducible levers. Generic heuristics transfer poorly, competition can erode gains, and citation-oriented rewrites can impair retrieval. Commercial audits reveal low source overlap and persistent fidelity gaps. Proposes reproducible protocol: repeated measurements, paraphrases, controls, human validation.
