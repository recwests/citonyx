## Summary
AI models have a 'never_search' trigger for generalized knowledge — Claude's leaked system prompt shows it doesn't search for 'timeless or stable' information. Princeton study (Narasimhan) found quotes and statistics give 30-40% visibility uplift. Date and number entity types predict ChatGPT citations most.
## Details
If your content only restates consensus, RAG skips you — the model uses its training data instead of searching. Eric Lancheres found unique data points as the strongest ranking predictor for AI citations. Kevin Indig confirmed date and number entity types as top predictors for ChatGPT citations specifically. Practical application: every page needs at least one unique data point, proprietary statistic, or original quote. Content that only synthesizes common knowledge has near-zero AI retrieval value. Original research, surveys, and proprietary datasets remain the highest-ROI GEO tactic for this reason.

**Update (2026-07-18):** The Princeton IF-GEO framework (Aggarwal et al., KDD 2024) formally quantifies Information Gain as explaining 73% of citation variance across all tested models — more than domain authority or backlinks. The GEO-bench benchmark was introduced alongside (2,500+ queries, 100K+ pages). The Information Gain framework is now the foundational theoretical model for GEO, operationalized by vendors achieving 94% citation prediction accuracy.
