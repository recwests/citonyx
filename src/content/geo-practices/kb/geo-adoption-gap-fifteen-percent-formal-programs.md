## Summary
67% of Fortune 500 CMOs now name GEO a top-three digital priority for FY26 (up from 18% in 2024). Yet fewer than 15% of marketing teams have formal GEO programs with defined citation tracking, regular measurement cadences, and AI-citation-shaped content strategy. Only 16% of brands systematically track AI search performance. The GEO services market is projected to grow from ~$850M-$1.5B to $17-20B by 2034 at CAGRs of 34-50%.

## Details
McKinsey projects ~$750B in US revenue will flow through AI-powered search channels by 2028. Enterprise AI adoption jumped from 55% to 78% in a single year. 92% of Fortune 500 companies now use OpenAI's generative AI. Agentic AI: 23% of organizations scaling in at least one function, 39% actively experimenting. Gartner forecasts 40% of enterprise applications will embed task-specific AI agents by end of 2026. The teams furthest behind are those treating GEO as a future priority — the window for first-mover advantage closes as AI citation surfaces consolidate around established brands.
