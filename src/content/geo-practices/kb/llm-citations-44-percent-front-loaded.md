## Summary
LLMs disproportionately extract citations from the beginning of content. Growth Memo found 44.2% of all LLM citations come from the first 30% of text (introduction), 31.1% from middle, 24.7% from conclusion. Pages with claim-rich introductions get cited 2.1x more frequently.

## Details
The Princeton GEO framework identified Statistics Addition and Cite Sources as top-performing techniques, boosting visibility up to 40%. Practical application: front-load factual claims, statistics, and quotable definitions within the first 100 words. Place direct-answer blocks (1-3 sentences) within first 120 words. Structure headings as full grammatical questions. This pattern consistently increases AI citation probability across ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews. Pages structured for LLM extraction get cited 4-8x more than pages optimized for classic Google ranking factors.
