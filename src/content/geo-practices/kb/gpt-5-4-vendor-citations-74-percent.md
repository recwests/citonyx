## Summary
GPT-5.4 cites vendor/first-party content at 74.6% of citations vs other models citing ~79% third-party. The newer model shifts toward rewarding brands with well-structured, authoritative own-site content — a reversal of the broader third-party citation trend.

## Details
2,391 citations analyzed across GPT-5.0, GPT-5.4, Gemini 3, and Claude 4.5. If the trend continues toward first-party sourcing, brands with high-quality own-site content, clear schema structure, and entity clarity will benefit from newer AI models. This suggests GEO investment in own-site content quality has growing ROI as models improve retrieval capabilities. However, this is an early signal from one study — most other research still shows third-party dominance. The implication: own-site content optimization is a hedge that pays off as models evolve.
