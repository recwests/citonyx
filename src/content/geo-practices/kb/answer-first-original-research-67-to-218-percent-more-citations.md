## Summary
6-month study (Sep 2025–Feb 2026) of 1,000 websites across 24 industries, 50K+ queries, 180K+ citations. Answer-first structure (direct answer in first 100-150 words) = 67% more citations. Original research = 218% more. Content under 3 months = 182% more. FAQ pages = 89% more. Author credentials + named attribution = 43% more (Texta, Mar 2026).

## Details
Comprehensive regression analysis: original research (β=0.38) was the strongest predictor, followed by freshness (β=-0.31), answer-first structure (β=0.28), FAQ inclusion (β=0.24), and author credentials (β=0.19). Multi-author content receives 34% more citations than single-author. For health content, author credentials lift citations by 67%. Schema markup correlates with 34% higher citation rate.
