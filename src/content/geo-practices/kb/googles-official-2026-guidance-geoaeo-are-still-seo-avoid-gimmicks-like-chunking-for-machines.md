## Summary
Google published its first official guide to optimizing for generative AI features in May 2026. Core message: GEO and AEO are 'still SEO' — built on the same crawlable, trustworthy foundations as traditional search. Explicitly warns against gimmicks like content 'chunking' for machines or unnecessary AI text files (llms.txt). 'Helpful, accurate, original content remains the strategy that compounds.' Advice: target answer formats (concise definitions, comparisons, FAQs, lists, tables) but keep it human-first.

## Details
Google's guidance clarifies that AI visibility is not a separate optimization discipline requiring special tricks. The fundamentals that work for traditional Google Search — crawlability, content quality, authority, E-E-A-T — are the same fundamentals that earn AI citations. Specific anti-patterns called out: creating llms.txt files without other optimization (does nothing for AI Overviews eligibility), artificially chunking content for AI extraction, and writing robotic answer-optimized text. Google's message: serve humans first, AI second — they reward the same signals.
