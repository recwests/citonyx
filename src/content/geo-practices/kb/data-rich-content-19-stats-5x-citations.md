## Summary
The only peer-reviewed GEO study (Princeton/Georgia Tech/IIT Delhi, KDD 2024): 6 strategies tested across 10 search engines, 10,000 queries. Statistics addition = +41% visibility, quotations = +28%, citing external sources = +115% for lower-ranked pages. Simply adding more words = no improvement. SE Ranking's independent study of 129,000 domains: pages with 19+ statistical data points averaged 5.4 ChatGPT citations vs 2.8 for data-light pages. Effect strongest for law, government, and opinion queries.

## Details
ZipTie.dev confirms data-rich pages earn nearly double AI citations. Data improvement of +37-41% on Position-Adjusted Word Count. Critical for challenger brands: lower-ranked sites (position 5+) improved visibility by 115% when optimized, while position-1 sites decreased by ~30%. GEO optimization disproportionately helps underdogs. 68.7% of ChatGPT-cited pages follow strict H1→H2→H3 hierarchy. Self-contained passages (134-167 words) cited 2.3x more frequently. GEO market projected at $365.4M in 2026 (42.9% CAGR). 92% of marketers plan AI search optimization but only 40.6% do it. 57% of B2B brands are in low consistency bracket (<30% citation on 2+ platforms) per Alexandrya benchmarks. Three brands capture 51% of all AI citations in Marketing Technology. Perplexity specifically: 19+ statistics, comparison tables, definitive answer capsules within first 400 words achieve 4.1x higher citation rates. Statistics Addition remains highest-impact single modification per the Princeton GEO paper.
