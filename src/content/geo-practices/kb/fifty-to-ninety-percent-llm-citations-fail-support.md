## Summary
Nature Communications research (Wu et al., 2025) found 50-90% of LLM-generated citations don't fully support their claims. Best-performing platform accuracy hits ~66%, worst falls below 50%. Users hover ~2 sources on AI answers vs 12 on traditional search — verifying less while trust is higher. This trust asymmetry is structurally dangerous for brand citations.

## Details
The SourceCheckup evaluation framework achieved 88.7% agreement with medical expert evaluators. Cloudflare data shows AI crawlers consume content 38,000x more than they refer traffic back — creating a perverse incentive where content is consumed for citation but not rewarded with traffic. Only 40.4% of HealthSearchQA responses had complete citation support on human validation. The finding has direct GEO implications: being cited is not enough — brands must ensure the citation actually represents their content accurately. Citation support quality is an under-monitored KPI. The gap between user trust (high) and actual citation accuracy (low) means incorrect citations do more brand damage than uncited alternatives.
