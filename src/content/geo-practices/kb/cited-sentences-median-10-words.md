## Summary
AI models extract short, front-loaded sentences for citation. Study of 153K citations across 6 platforms: median cited sentence is 10 words, nothing over 18 words cited. Content before first image/table has highest citation probability.

## Details
GitHub study (May 2026) across Google AI Mode, Grok, Gemini, Copilot, Perplexity, ChatGPT. Gemini expanded fragment coverage to 84.1% — only remaining window into Google's sentence-level chunking. Google AI Mode dropped text fragment exposure entirely (0% from 70.9% in March). Sentences > 18 words effectively never cited. Positional bias: content before the first image or table has significantly higher citation probability. Readability matters: Flesch scores correlate with citation likelihood. Pages with FCP < 0.4s average 6.7 AI citations vs 2.1 for slow pages. Practical takeaway: lead with concise, data-rich claims in the first third of the page.
