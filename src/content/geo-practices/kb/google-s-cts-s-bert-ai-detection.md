## Summary
Google's S-CTS (Site Classifier Termination System) terminates entire content networks rather than individual pages. S-BERT detects the mathematical fingerprint of AI text — paraphrasing and switch tools provide no protection. LoRA + APO updates detect new AI models within days of release.

## Details
This represents a significant escalation in Google's AI content detection capability. S-CTS operates at the network level: if Google detects AI-generated content patterns across multiple sites owned by the same entity, it terminates the entire network simultaneously. S-BERT analyzes semantic fingerprints that are inherent to AI-generated text — rewriting or paraphrasing does not evade detection. The LoRA and APO update mechanisms allow Google to adapt to new AI models within days. For GEO practitioners: AI-assisted content with substantial human editing is safe; fully automated content faces existential ranking risk.
