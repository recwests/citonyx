## Summary
Google stated plainly in 2026 that no AI-specific markup, special schema, or llms.txt is required for AI Overviews or AI Mode. In Google's own words, 'GEO is still SEO at the core.' Standard structured data helps parsability but proprietary AI markup solutions are unnecessary.

## Details
This official Google statement (2026) debunks the GEO myth that special AI markup files are required. Google explicitly said no AI-specific requirements exist. The official developers.google.com guide (July 2026) elaborates: llms.txt and other 'AI text files' — Google doesn't use them. No requirement to 'chunk' content into tiny pieces. No need to write specifically for AI systems. May 2026: spam policies explicitly include attempts to manipulate generative AI responses. Ahrefs: only 3% of 137K llms.txt implementations get any traffic; AI retrieval bots = 1.1% of all crawler requests. However, structured data still helps parsability and citation accuracy. GPT-4 accuracy jumps from 16% to 54% when content has standard structured data (Data World study). The takeaway: invest in clean semantic HTML, standard schema.org types, and server-side rendering rather than chasing proprietary AI markup solutions.
