## Summary
Indexably's combined model of 18,129 cited pages and 4,622 controls shows domain-level factors account for 77% of predictive importance vs 23% for page factors. Backlink diversity (referring subnets, d=0.513) beats backlink volume (d=0.098). Page optimization only lifts citations for top-quartile authority domains.
## Details
Logistic regression combining page and domain signals: DataForSEO Rank (d=1.075) is 7x stronger than any single page signal. Page factors only show positive effects in the top authority quartile (d=0.24-0.37). For everyone else, effects are flat or slightly negative — page optimization compounds with authority, doesn't substitute for it. Basic HTML structure (doctype, lang, canonical, viewport, meta description) is the strongest page-level differentiator — not schema, FAQ format, or word count. Cited pages have higher vocabulary diversity, shorter paragraphs, and shorter max sections. Each AI model has distinct preferences: ChatGPT favors freshness/metadata, Claude spreads evenly, Gemini values crawlability, AI Overviews is most balanced.
