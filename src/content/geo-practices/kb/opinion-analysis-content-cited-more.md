## Summary
AI engines cite opinion and analysis pieces more frequently than neutral or purely descriptive content. A strong point of view supported by evidence provides a clear, extractable citation reason. Balanced neutrality is less cited because it offers less definitive reference material.

## Details
Aligned with the finding that ChatGPT prefers definite language over hedging, AI systems gravitate toward content that takes a position. Analysis pieces that interpret data, make predictions, or offer expert judgment are cited as authoritative references. This reverses the traditional SEO preference for neutral, comprehensive content. Trigger-event strategy amplifies this: publishing analysis when a topic is trending increases citation probability. Interactive tools and calculators are also cited as reference resources — they provide utility that AI cannot replicate. For the Citonyx testbed: publish data-backed opinion and analysis, not just neutral descriptions. Time publication to industry events and trends.
