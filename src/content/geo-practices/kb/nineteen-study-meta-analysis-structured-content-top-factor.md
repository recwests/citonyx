## Summary
Organic Labs synthesized 19 independent studies plus 6 case studies covering 10,000+ LLM responses. Top factors by correlation: Structured content/FAQs/Schema (r=0.81), Comprehensive content (r=0.79), Brand mentions/PR (r=0.76), Knowledge Graph (r=0.49), E-E-A-T (r=0.49), UGC/Community (r=0.54), Freshness (r=0.34), Rapid Indexing (r=0.42). Content volume (word count) and backlinks show no meaningful correlation with AI citation.

## Details
Prompt injection techniques scored only r=0.17 — not viable long-term. The key finding: traditional SEO factors (word count r=0.12, backlinks r=0.09 per Alexandrya data) do not transfer to answer systems. C-SEO Bench arXiv study confirmed conventional SEO tactics ineffective on generative search. Self-contained passages of 134-167 words cited 2.3x more frequently. For practitioners: the meta-analysis provides an evidence-weighted priority list — fix content structure and comprehensiveness before chasing links or volume. Community/UGC at r=0.54 outperforms freshness and indexing — a counterintuitive finding worth further investigation.
