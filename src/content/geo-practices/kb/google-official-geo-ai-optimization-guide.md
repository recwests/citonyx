## Summary
Google's official AI optimization guide published at Google I/O 2026. Key positions: no special file, hidden markup, or separate protocol for generative AI. GEO/AEO framed as 'terms you may see used' — from Google's perspective it's still SEO. llms.txt? Google doesn't use it. Content chunking? Not required. Special AI schema? No separate protocol needed.

## Details
Ahrefs data confirms only 3% of llms.txt implementations get any traffic; AI retrieval bots = 1.1% of requests (Slackbot fetched more than PerplexityBot). Google recommends: standard SEO best practices, non-commodity expert-led content, clear technical structure. Warns against: inauthentic mentions, fake best-of lists, recommendation poisoning, scaled doorway pages, schema contradicting visible content. May 2026 spam policies now explicitly cover attempts to manipulate generative AI responses. The practical implication: invest in SEO fundamentals before GEO-specific tactics.
